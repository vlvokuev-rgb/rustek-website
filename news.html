Kazakhstan · Silleno

Silleno Remains the Strongest Current Kazakhstan Project-Led Opportunity

Among the current Kazakhstan projects in Rustek's focus area, Silleno Polyethylene remains the strongest project-led opportunity for foreign suppliers of pressure equipment, Ex-related equipment and documentation-heavy packages. Current internal mapping indicates confirmed EPC context with Tecnicas Reunidas / Sinopec, with the best entry routes through EPC and direct supplier contact.

The most relevant package clusters currently include:

  • pressure vessels, separators and heat exchangers;
  • pressure-containing skids and package units;
  • Ex electrical panels and hazardous-area packages;
  • documentation-heavy imported packages.

For suppliers involved in such packages, early review of certification route, technical passports and project documentation requirements can help reduce tender-stage uncertainty and avoid delays later in execution.

Kazakhstan · EAEU

Kazakhstan: Grey Certificates Remain a Practical Risk for EAEU Supplies

Kazakhstan has stepped up enforcement against grey certificates and other conformity assessment documents issued with procedural violations in EAEU member states. This means that low-cost or purely formal certification routes used outside Kazakhstan may create practical risks if the resulting documents are later challenged or invalidated.

For foreign suppliers, the key point is not the country of issue alone, but whether the certification route has been carried out properly and can withstand scrutiny in the target market.

Our approach: we help clients choose a certification route that is commercially workable and technically defensible for Kazakhstan project supplies.
